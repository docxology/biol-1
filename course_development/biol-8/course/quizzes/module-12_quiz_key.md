## Part B: Free Response (3 points)

**8. Sliding Filament Theory (1 point)**
- Calcium is released from sarcoplasmic reticulum
- Calcium binds to troponin, exposing actin binding sites
- Myosin heads bind to actin forming cross-bridges
- ATP hydrolysis powers the power stroke, pulling actin toward center
- Sarcomere shortens as thin filaments slide over thick filaments
- Cycle repeats as long as calcium and ATP are present

**9. ATP in Muscle Contraction (1 point)**
- ATP binds to myosin head causing it to release from actin
- ATP hydrolysis cocks the myosin head for the next power stroke
- ATP is needed to pump calcium back into sarcoplasmic reticulum (relaxation)
- Without ATP, muscles cannot contract or relax (rigor mortis)
