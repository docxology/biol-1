Module 12: Gene Expression (DNA to Protein)

Comprehension & Critical Thinking Questions

Part 1: Core Concepts

  1. DNA Structure

    • Describe the double helix. What forms the backbone? What forms the rungs?
    • Explain antiparallel orientation (5′ to 3′).
    • State Chargaff's Rules. If a DNA sample is 20% Adenine, what percent is Cytosine?

  2. The Central Dogma

    • Diagram the flow: DNA → RNA → Protein.
    • Where does transcription occur? Where does translation occur?

  3. The Machinery

    • What is the role of DNA polymerase? RNA polymerase? Ribosomes?

Part 2: Application

  1. Replication

    • DNA replication is semi-conservative. Explain what this means.
    • Why is there a leading strand (continuous synthesis) and a lagging strand (Okazaki fragments)?

  2. Translation Practice

    • DNA template strand: 3′-TAC GGG AAA ACT-5′.
    • Transcribe to mRNA (5′→3′).
    • Translate to amino acids using a codon chart.

Part 3: Analysis & Evaluation

  1. RNA Processing

    • Explain the purpose of:
      • Introns vs. Exons
      • 5′ Cap and Poly-A Tail
    • Why might alternative splicing be advantageous?

  2. Universal Code

    • The genetic code is nearly universal. How does this support common ancestry? How does it enable biotechnology (e.g., producing human insulin in bacteria)?
